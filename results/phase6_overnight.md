# Phase 6 — overnight-derived per-user sensitivity

110 users with ≥60 nocturnal (00:00–06:00) clean windows.

## Level

- median overnight ISF **18.4** vs entered 51.0 vs all-day empirical 21.6 mg/dL/U
- overnight/entered ratio median **0.39** (all-day empirical/entered was ~0.41 — i.e. overnight sits still below the entered value)

## Reproducibility (14-day block test-retest)

- overnight block-to-block CV: **±50%** (all-day was ±34%) — similar

## Decisive bias test (Phase-5 directional test on the OVERNIGHT ISF)

| association | ρ (overnight) | all-day (Phase 5) | expected |
|---|---|---|---|
| logR vs TAR | +0.01 | +0.00 | + |
| logR vs TBR | +0.39 | +0.38 | − |

## Reading

- The hypo-bias **persists overnight** (logR-vs-TBR +0.39): restricting to nocturnal windows does not de-bias the sensitivity estimate; overnight alone does not solve the Phase-5 problem.

*Caveat:* dawn drift partly absorbed by the trend covariate but not eliminated; overnight ΔIOB is smaller (post-dinner tail), so per-user n is lower; observational.
